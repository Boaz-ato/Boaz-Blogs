Welcome to Boaz's Blog

This blog is where I explore ideas in quantum computing, machine learning, and physics that I find interesting. Rather than skimming the surface, I like to dig into the mathematical details, working through each derivation step by step so the reasoning is transparent. I hope you enjoy the blogs!

  • Quantum Error Correction

    This series explains quantum error correction starting from the basics through to current research, discussing the surface code and qLDPC codes. I will also discuss how we can assess the performance of quantum codes using STIM. Anyone with a basic understanding of quantum computing and linear algebra can follow along.

  • Variational Autoencoders

    Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) are a class of generative models that learn a latent space from data. This series derives the VAE from first principles, starting with the evidence lower bound (ELBO), working through the reparameterisation trick that makes the model trainable, and arriving at the full objective. We then implement a VAE.

  • Diffusion Models

    Diffusion models are the generative framework behind state-of-the-art image synthesis systems such as Stable Diffusion. This series develops the mathematical formulation covering the forward noising process, the reverse denoising objective, score matching, and the connection to stochastic differential equations. We implement a diffusion model in code and explore how diffusion models can be used for molecular docking, where the model learns to predict how a drug molecule binds to a protein target.

  • Jordan Algebra

    Jordan algebras are a class of non-associative algebraic structures originally introduced by physicist Pascual Jordan in an attempt to find an algebraic foundation for quantum mechanics. In quantum theory, physical observables are represented by Hermitian operators, and the standard matrix product of two Hermitian operators is not itself Hermitian. Jordan algebras resolve this by replacing the matrix product with the symmetrised Jordan product. This makes them a natural language for quantum observables.

  • Stochastic Gradient Descent

    Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) is the workhorse optimisation algorithm behind virtually all of modern machine learning. This series builds the theory from the ground up — starting from gradient descent on convex functions, introducing the stochastic approximation that makes it scale to large datasets, and analysing convergence. We then cover the key variants that power deep learning in practice: momentum, RMSProp, and Adam, with a focus on the intuition behind each modification and when to use it.

  • Data Structures and Algorithms

    A rigorous series on data structures and algorithms, covering the fundamental building blocks that underpin all of software engineering. We work through arrays, linked lists, trees, heaps, hash tables, and graphs, then turn to the algorithmic techniques — sorting, searching, dynamic programming, greedy methods, and graph traversal — analysing the time and space complexity of each. The goal is to build both the intuition and the formal reasoning needed to design efficient solutions from scratch.
